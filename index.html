Suggested route

Flexible order. If Paris is cheaper, start in France. If Amsterdam or Brussels drops, reverse the route. Trains make this triangle easy.

Days 1–4

Paris

Recover from flight, Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Montmartre, Seine walk, cafés.

Days 5–6

Brussels + Bruges

Grand Place, waffles, chocolate, day trip to Bruges canals.

Days 7–10

Amsterdam

Canals, Rijksmuseum, Anne Frank House, Jordaan, cozy winter cafés.

Days 11–13

France side trip

Versailles, Lyon, Strasbourg, or Loire Valley depending on vibe and trains.

Days 14–15

Buffer + fly home

Keep a final buffer day near the departure city. Future Davi approves.

Cool places by country

A starter map: enough signal to plan, not a 94-tab travel rabbit hole.

🇳🇱

Netherlands

  • Amsterdam canals: best explored slowly, especially Jordaan.
  • Rijksmuseum + Van Gogh Museum: book timed tickets early.
  • Anne Frank House: emotional, essential; tickets sell out fast.
  • Zaanse Schans: windmills and classic Dutch scenery near Amsterdam.
  • Utrecht: quieter canal city; great day trip.
🇫🇷

France

  • Paris classics: Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Musée d’Orsay, Notre-Dame area.
  • Montmartre: Sacré-Cœur, street artists, sunset views.
  • Versailles: palace day trip; winter crowds are kinder.
  • Strasbourg or Colmar: Alsace winter charm, half-timbered streets.
  • Food mission: croissants, steak-frites, crêpes, cheese, wine.
🇧🇪

Belgium

  • Brussels Grand Place: stunning at night.
  • Bruges: medieval streets, canals, chocolate shops.
  • Ghent: cooler, less crowded, beautiful old town.
  • Atomium: weird and iconic — worth a photo stop.
  • Food mission: waffles, fries, chocolate, beer tasting.
